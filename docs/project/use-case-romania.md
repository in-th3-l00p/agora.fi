# use case: Romanian Tech Space

The first production Space on AGORAFI, focused on the Romanian startup ecosystem.

## overview

| | |
|-|-|

The Romanian Tech Space brings together the country's startup ecosystem — from the major tech hubs of Bucharest, Cluj, Timișoara, and Iași — into a single, governed, economy-driven virtual environment.

## target audience

- **Romanian startups and founders** — showcase their companies, gain visibility, connect with peers
- **Tech communities** — local meetup groups, developer communities, university programs
- **Investors** — VCs and angels focused on Eastern European markets, looking for deal flow
- **Accelerators** — Techcelerator, How to Web, Startarium, and similar programs
- **Government innovation programs** — visibility for grants, regulatory updates, public-private partnerships

## sub-spaces

The Romanian Tech Space includes specialized areas within the grid:

### News Factory
AI-generated news feed tracking Romanian tech funding rounds, product launches, exits, and partnerships. Tile owners can also post their own announcements. Categories: Funding, Partnerships, Launches, Events, Awards.

### Innovation Gallery
A showcase area for featured Romanian startups. Hosts product demos, virtual demo days, and startup spotlights.

### Hackathon Arena
Integration with local events — How to Web hackathons, Startarium competitions, university coding challenges. Teams compete and results are published on-chain.

### Investor Lounge
A discovery hub for VCs and angels. Browse startup tiles, review pitch materials, track deal flow, and attend virtual pitch events.

### Regional Hubs
Dedicated districts for Romania's major tech cities:
- **Bucharest** — the capital's startup scene
- **Cluj** — the growing tech hub of Transylvania
- **Timișoara** — Western Romania's innovation corridor
- **Iași** — Eastern Romania's university-driven tech community

### Government Plaza
Visibility for government innovation programs, grants, regulatory updates, and public-private partnership opportunities.

## launch strategy

### phase 1: Genesis (weeks 1–2)

- 100 genesis tiles available for early adopters
- Founding members receive a soulbound "Founder Badge" NFT
- Airdrop of 10,000 $ROTECH tokens to each founder
- Launch partnerships with Techcelerator and How to Web

### phase 2: Public Launch (weeks 3–4)

- Remaining 900 tiles open to the public
- AI News Factory goes live
- First DAO proposals submitted
- Community events and onboarding campaigns

### phase 3: Growth (months 2–6)

- Staking program launches
- Rental marketplace activates
- Partnerships with Romanian VCs and angel networks
- Integration with local tech events and conferences
